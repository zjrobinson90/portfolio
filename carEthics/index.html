carEthics

Self-driving cars need to make decisions about what to do in different situations. Some of those situations might involve the life of a human. But who should be making those decisions? Programmers, CEOs, our President?
Ethics is a tricky question with no definite right or wrong answer and ends up being whatever the majority thinks.
So, I think the morality of our self-driving cars' decisions should also be left up to the majority.
This is a little game to crowd-source the data for those decisions. This is by no means an exhaustive list of possibilities, but rather an interesting thought experiment for how this data collection method could go.

Instructions
There are three options. The option on top, the option on the bottom, and the option to drive into a wall and kill yourself.
Your responsibility is to pick whichever option you want your self-driving car to take.
At the end, you'll see your results as a bar graph showing the number of instance in which you selected a person (or persons) to kill.
